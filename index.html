Research Experiences

Dialogue State Tracking (DST)

2018.6-2020.1

In task-oriented conversational systems, dialogue state tracking refers to the problem of estimating a user’s goals and requests at each turn of a dialogue. The state is typically defined by the underlying ontology of the domains represented in a dialogue, and a system’s job is to learn accurate distributions for the values of certain domain-specific slots in the ontology.

Spoken Language Understanding (SLU)

Spoken language understanding is the task of inferring the semantic meaning of spoken utterances, which is an essential component of voice assistants, social bots, and intelligent home devices.

Machine Reading Comprehension (MRC)

2020.9-2021.9

Machine reading comprehension is a challenging task which teaches machine to read and comprehend a given passage/paragraph and answer its corresponding questions. It is also one of the long-term goals of natural language understanding, and has important applications in e.g., building intelligent agents for conversation and customer service support.

Projects

Transfer Learning Based Large Scale Cross-Domain Spoken Language Understanding
  • Built a robust system to tackle two dominated SLU tasks (intent detection & named entity recognition) in response to the cross-domain corpus.
  • Used the recurrent neural network~(RNN) as the encoder and experimented with several structures, such as the attention mechanism and RNN decoder.
  • Constructed a multi-task framework, with shared parameters to learn domain-common knowledge and independent parameters for each domain to learn domain-specific knowledge.
  • Used several multi-task training strategies to enhanced the system, including adversarial training and meta-learning.
Few-Shot Learning in Spoken Language Understanding
  • The Evaluation of Chinese Human-Computer Dialogue Technology Competition Task 1, 9th National Conference on Social Media Processing (SMP2020-ECDT). Finally, we achieved 2nd place on the leaderboard.
  • Designed a series of SLU algorithms training with the given large-scale data and adapting unseen domain data by fine-tuning on small-scale samples (i.e., few-shot learning).
  • Built a universal SLU model with Lattice layer to enhance the encoder by adapting Chinese characteristics. Improved the performance with additional bi-translation and syniny-based data augmentation.
Multiple Intent Classification in Real-World Dialogue
  • Collaborate with China Pacific Life Insurance (CPIC). The data are real-world dialogues collected by the staff service of CPIC.
  • Built an audio-based intelligent service to detect the intents of customers under real-world dialogue scenes.
  • Used an efficient ASR module to convert audio data to textual data with ASR errors. Collected the textual data without ASR errors by artificial annotation. Built a textual data-oriented intent detection model with Roberta as the backbone and linear classifier to detect the most suitable intent in candidates. Evaluations of both data with and without ASR errors are achieved to predefined criteria.
  • Used layer-wise decay training strategy to fine-tune pretrained Bert. Enhanced the encoding ability of Chinese with Lattice layer. Enhanced the intent classifier with label smoothing. Used PGD and FGM adversarial training strategies to improve the robustness of the system.
Chinese Multi-Choice Machine Reading Comprehension Challenge
  • The final project of the natural language processing course for graduated students. Finally, we achieved the highest accuracy in the class.
  • Built an efficient MRC system to tackle the multi-choice MRC task. Used MacBert as the backbone and cross-attention mechanism based classifier to score among candidates.
  • Used layer-wise decay training strategy to fine-tune pretrained MacBert. Used label smoothing to enhance the classifier. Used adversarial training loss to improve the robustness of the MRC system.
Grammar-Enhanced Multi-Task Text-to-SQL Framework
  • Used RGAT layer to fuse the question and database schema and a tree-based syntax-guided decoder to generate the SQL sketch in the first stage.
  • Used T5 decoder to generate grammatically incorrect SQL according to the sketch in the second stage.
  • Construct the system in a multi-task framework to enhance the model and decrease the mass of the system by training with sketch generation loss and SQL generation loss jointly.
